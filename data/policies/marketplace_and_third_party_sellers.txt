NORTHLAKE COMMERCE — MARKETPLACE AND THIRD-PARTY SELLER POLICY
Effective: 2026-01-01.

SECTION 1 — MARKETPLACE MODEL
1.1 Third-party sellers list items on Northlake Marketplace. Northlake provides the platform; sellers are responsible for their listings unless Northlake is the seller of record.

1.2 “Fulfilled by Northlake” programs may apply different return and refund rules as labeled.

SECTION 2 — BUYER PROTECTION
2.1 If an item is not received or is materially not as described, open a case within the case window shown on the order.

2.2 Evidence may include photos, expert assessments, and chat transcripts.

SECTION 3 — SELLER PERFORMANCE
3.1 Sellers must meet shipping time and cancellation rate thresholds or face listing limits.

SECTION 4 — AUTHENTICITY
4.1 Counterfeit claims require manufacturer letters or authorized authenticator results when disputed.

SECTION 5 — RETURNS
5.1 Seller return policies are displayed at checkout; Northlake baseline protection may override seller policies in specific jurisdictions.

SECTION 6 — PAYOUTS AND HOLDS
6.1 Seller funds may be held during investigations.

SECTION 7 — PROHIBITED LISTINGS
7.1 Hazardous materials without permits, recalled products, and IP-infringing goods are prohibited.

SECTION 8 — EDGE CASES
E1: Seller ships empty box: buyer protection applies; seller penalized.

E2: Partial refund negotiated: both parties must agree in platform flow.

E3: Cross-border seller: duties may surprise buyer; disclosures required.

E4: Used-like-new disputes: grading standards referenced to listing text.

E5: Dropship delays: seller responsible unless force majeure proven.

E6: Seller asks to cancel for cheaper elsewhere: unethical; report.

E7: Buyer threatens negative review for refund: policy violation.

E8: Combined cart marketplace + first-party: returns split per seller rules.

E9: Seller bankruptcy: buyer protection may cover eligible claims.

E10: VAT invoices from sellers: request via invoice tool.

Escalate fraud rings to Marketplace Integrity.
