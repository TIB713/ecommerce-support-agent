NORTHLAKE COMMERCE — FRAUD PREVENTION AND ACCOUNT INTEGRITY POLICY
Effective: 2026-01-01.

SECTION 1 — PURPOSE
This policy explains how we detect and respond to fraud, account takeover, abusive returns, and collusion between buyers and sellers on our marketplace.

SECTION 2 — VERIFICATION
2.1 We may request identity verification for high-risk orders, changed shipping addresses, or unusual purchase velocity.

2.2 Failure to complete verification may lead to order cancellation.

SECTION 3 — ACCOUNT RESTRICTIONS
3.1 Temporary holds may apply while we investigate chargebacks, return anomalies, or promo abuse.

3.2 Permanent closure may occur for severe violations, subject to appeal processes where required by law.

SECTION 4 — MARKETPLACE FRAUD
4.1 Sellers listing counterfeit goods may be suspended and funds held pending investigation.

4.2 Buyers colluding with sellers to extract refunds may be prosecuted where applicable.

SECTION 5 — CHARGEBACK AND RETURN ABUSE
5.1 “Item not received” claims contradicted by carrier evidence may lead to account flags.

5.2 Bracketing purchases (buying many variants to return most) may lose free returns privileges.

SECTION 6 — DEVICE AND IP SIGNALS
6.1 Automated risk models use device fingerprints and IP reputation as signals, not sole determinants.

SECTION 7 — APPEALS
7.1 You may submit documentation via the appeal portal. Decisions typically within ten (10) business days.

SECTION 8 — LAW ENFORCEMENT
8.1 We cooperate with lawful requests and may preserve records.

SECTION 9 — EDGE CASES
E1: Gift purchase flagged as fraud; purchaser must verify.

E2: VPN usage triggers step-up auth.

E3: New account large electronics order: common verification path.

E4: Shared household accounts: mixed signals resolved via identity proof.

E5: Stolen card reported after shipment: follow chargeback process.

E6: Account takeover after SIM swap: recovery via backup codes and support.

E7: Seller coercion reviews: fraud team investigates patterns.

E8: Coupon farms: bulk account creation blocked.

E9: Refund mules: shipping to freight forwarders may be restricted.

E10: High return rate in apparel try-on abuse: restocking fees applied.

Escalate to Trust & Safety for coordinated inauthentic behavior.
