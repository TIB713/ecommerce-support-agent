NORTHLAKE COMMERCE — DAMAGED, DEFECTIVE, AND MISSING ITEMS POLICY
Effective: 2026-01-01.

SECTION 1 — REPORTING WINDOW
1.1 Report shipping damage within forty-eight (48) hours of delivery for priority handling. Reports after seven (7) days may require additional evidence.

1.2 Missing items must be reported within seventy-two (72) hours of delivery scan for high-value orders; standard orders within seven (7) days.

SECTION 2 — EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS
2.1 Provide photos of the outer shipping box, inner packaging, damage to the product, and the shipping label.

2.2 For missing accessories, photos of everything received and the sealed areas of packaging if applicable.

2.3 Serial number photos for electronics when serial tracking is used.

SECTION 3 — RESOLUTION OPTIONS
3.1 Minor cosmetic box damage with intact product may receive a small courtesy credit instead of replacement if you accept.

3.2 Functional defects within the return window may be replaced, repaired under warranty, or refunded per your preference and inventory.

3.3 Missing items may be reshipped or refunded if investigation confirms short shipment.

SECTION 4 — CARRIER INVESTIGATIONS
4.1 If tracking shows delivered but you claim non-receipt, we open a carrier case. Outcomes may take up to ten (10) business days.

4.2 If signature proof exists at your address, denial of receipt may require a police report for replacement above a threshold (e.g., one thousand dollars).

SECTION 3A — THIRD-PARTY MARKETPLACE
3A.1 Seller-fulfilled damage claims route to the seller first. If the seller is unresponsive within forty-eight (48) hours, our marketplace team may intervene.

SECTION 4 — FOOD AND PERISHABLES
4.1 Spoilage claims require photos and may require return of remaining product where safe.

SECTION 5 — LARGE APPLIANCES
5.1 Delivery damage must be noted on the delivery receipt at time of delivery when possible. If not noted, claims are harder but not impossible with strong evidence.

SECTION 6 — PARTIAL SHIPMENT ERRORS
6.1 If you ordered quantity three and received two, we verify warehouse scan data and CCTV sampling when disputes persist.

SECTION 7 — FRAUD SIGNALS
7.1 Repeated missing-item claims across accounts may trigger identity verification and temporary order holds.

SECTION 8 — WARRANTY OVERLAP
8.1 Manufacturing defects after the return window follow the Warranty Policy; shipping damage follows this policy within reporting windows.

SECTION 9 — EDGE CASES
E1: Concealed damage discovered after unboxing later than forty-eight hours: may still qualify with strong causal evidence linking to transit.

E2: Customer discards packaging then claims damage: harder to approve; may require inspection at service center.

E3: Accessory allegedly never included: we verify BOM lists; if SKU is supposed to include accessory, we ship accessory.

E4: Wrong item in sealed box: treated as fulfillment error; full replacement expedited.

E5: Item damaged by customer assembly error: not covered as shipping damage.

E6: Theft after delivery to a communal lobby: may be denied unless premium signature service was purchased.

E7: Carrier says delivered to back door without photo: investigation escalated.

E8: International damage claims require customs forms photos if box opened by customs.

E9: Battery swelling: safety protocol triggers replacement and return label for affected device.

E10: Multi-box shipment with one box missing: partial reship after carrier trace.

Escalate to Risk if patterns suggest abuse.
