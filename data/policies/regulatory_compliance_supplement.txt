NORTHLAKE COMMERCE — REGULATORY COMPLIANCE SUPPLEMENT (MULTI-JURISDICTION)
Effective: 2026-01-01. Supporting document for customer support interpretation.

PART A — CONSUMER RIGHTS OVERVIEWS (GENERAL)
This supplement provides internal-style guidance for how Northlake Commerce applies overlapping regulations. It does not constitute legal advice. Local law controls where it conflicts with internal policy.

A1 — Cooling-off rights: Some regions grant statutory withdrawal rights for distance sales within fourteen (14) days of delivery for eligible goods. Exceptions include sealed goods that cannot be returned for hygiene, perishable goods, and custom goods, mirroring themes in our Returns Policy.

A2 — Repair/replace remedies: Certain jurisdictions require repair or replacement for defects within a statutory period even if our discretionary return window closed. Escalate to Legal when customers cite specific statutes by name.

A3 — Accessibility: We provide reasonable accommodations for support access. Customers requesting alternate formats should be routed to Accessibility Services.

A4 — Product safety recalls: If a government agency announces a recall, follow the recall microsite instructions. Refunds may be expedited and may supersede restocking fees.

A5 — Environmental fees: Some electronics include recycling fees at checkout; refunds may include fee reversals proportional to returned items.

PART B — PAYMENTS AND ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING
B1 — High-value gift card purchases may trigger additional verification consistent with AML program rules.

B2 — Refunds to different payment instruments may be restricted to prevent laundering; escalate unusual routing requests.

PART C — EXPORT AND SANCTIONS
C1 — Orders to embargoed regions must be blocked. If a customer uses freight forwarders to circumvent, cancel and escalate.

C2 — Dual-use technology restrictions may require end-user statements before shipment.

PART D — TELECOM AND IOT DEVICES
D1 — Devices with radio modules must comply with regional certification marks; wrong region inventory cannot be substituted without customer consent.

PART E — FOOD AND SUPPLEMENTS
E1 — Health claims must align with labeling; support must not invent medical benefits not on packaging.

PART F — AUTOMOTIVE PARTS
F1 — Airbag and safety-critical parts may have additional return restrictions.

PART G — APPAREL FLAMMABILITY
G1 — Children’s sleepwear must meet flammability standards; recalls handled urgently.

PART H — SOFTWARE AND CONTENT
H1 — Age-gated content requires verified age in supported regions.

PART J — CROSS-POLICY CONFLICT RULES
J1 — If Shipping Policy timelines conflict with a statutory guarantee, the customer-benefiting rule applies where required.

J2 — If Promotions Policy conflicts with Refund Policy on proration, the promotion-specific disclosure at purchase controls if explicit.

J3 — If Marketplace Policy conflicts with Northlake first-party policy on the same order page, display precedence rules at checkout govern.

PART K — DOCUMENT CONTROL
K1 — Archived versions available upon request for disputes referencing historical terms.

K2 — Translation discrepancies: English version controls unless local law requires local language precedence.
