NORTHLAKE COMMERCE — GIFT CARDS, STORE CREDIT, AND PROMOTIONAL CREDITS
Effective: 2026-01-01.

SECTION 1 — GIFT CARD TYPES
1.1 Digital and physical gift cards are available where legally permitted.

1.2 Gift cards are not redeemable for cash except where required by law below certain balances.

SECTION 2 — EXPIRATION
2.1 Gift cards expire after five (5) years of inactivity in many jurisdictions; local rules may extend or shorten.

2.2 Promotional credits may expire sooner; expiration dates appear in account notifications.

SECTION 3 — LOSS AND THEFT
3.1 Treat gift cards like cash. Replacements require proof of purchase and may be denied if redeemed already.

SECTION 4 — FRAUDULENT PURCHASES
4.1 Gift cards purchased with stolen funds will be voided upon confirmation.

SECTION 5 — PARTIAL REDEMPTION
5.1 Balances remain on account until used or expired.

SECTION 6 — RETURNS PAID BY GIFT CARD
6.1 Refunds for orders paid by gift card return to gift card balance.

SECTION 7 — B2B INCENTIVES
7.1 Volume gift card purchases may require contractual terms and anti-resale clauses.

SECTION 8 — EDGE CASES
E1: Customer buys gift card with coupon where prohibited: order canceled.

E2: International gift card redemption blocked in unsupported regions.

E3: Split payment refund allocates per Refund Policy.

E4: Gift card used then chargeback on funding card: balance clawed back.

E5: Promotional credit not stackable with employee discount.

E6: Charity donation via gift card: special SKU rules.

E7: Lost email containing digital card: resend after verification.

E8: Physical card damaged unscannable: replacement after validation.

E9: Negative balance after partial chargeback: account limited until resolved.

E10: Merger and acquisition of brand: legacy cards honored per announcement.

Escalate high-value gift card disputes to Finance.
